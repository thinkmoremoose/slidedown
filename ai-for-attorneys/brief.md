# Agenda

**From AI Fundamentals to Operational Excellence**
This program provides a high-level educational roadmap for attorneys to understand, supervise, and operationalize artificial intelligence. By focusing on technical literacy and risk-management workflows, attendees will be armed with the knowledge to integrate these tools while strictly adhering to the Colorado Rules of Professional Conduct.

Part 1: The Engine Under the Hood (AI Fundamentals)
To supervise AI, you must first understand its "thought" process. We strip away the marketing jargon to explain the mechanics of the tools:

 * AI vs. LLMs: A primer on the distinction between general artificial intelligence and the Large Language Models currently dominating legal tech.
 * Vocabulary & Logic: Defining "tokens," "context windows," and "probabilistic output"—understanding that LLMs predict language rather than "knowing" facts.
 * Grounding & RAG: How "Retrieval-Augmented Generation" anchors an AI in trusted databases to prevent hallucinations.
 * The Velocity of Change: Strategies for conceptualizing the exponential rate of AI development and why "fixed" knowledge of a tool's capabilities expires quickly.

Part 2: The Ethical Compass (Competence & Supervision)
We translate technical risks into the framework of the Colorado Rules of Professional Conduct, arming attorneys with the "why" behind AI safeguards:
 * Technical Competence (Rule 1.1): What constitutes "sufficient knowledge" to use AI ethically in a legal matter?
 * Supervision (Rule 5.3): Treating AI as a non-lawyer assistant. We explore the duty to verify work product and the non-delegable nature of professional judgment.
 * Confidentiality (Rule 1.6): Identifying the "Data Leakage" points—how client information can inadvertently train public models and the critical importance of "Zero-Retention" environments.

Part 3: The Operational Blueprint (Value & Risk Management)
The final section provides the "how"—practical techniques to extract value while maintaining a defensive posture:
 * Risk-Adjusted Workflows: Designing "Human-in-the-Loop" (HITL) processes where the attorney handles the final authority.
 * Vetting Protocols: An approach for evaluating tech vendors on their security, grounding methods, and indemnity clauses.
 * Staying Current: How to build a "continuous learning" habit without being overwhelmed by the news cycle.
 * Framing with Real-World Scenarios: Using simple, relatable tasks to walk through exactly where the rewards manifest and where the ethical are hidden.

Educational Outcome:Attendees will leave with a clear vocabulary to evaluate new technologies, a framework for supervising AI assistants, and a strategy for evolving their practice alongside the technology.
